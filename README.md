# U.S. Affordability Analysis (BDI367)

## Project Overview
This project analyzes affordability across the United States by calculating an **expense-to-income ratio** using estimated living costs and wage data. The goal is to highlight how financial pressure varies across regions and family types, and to show that affordability challenges are not driven by income alone, but also by rising costs such as transportation, healthcare, and taxes.

## Key Metric
**Affordability Ratio = Total Yearly Expenses ÷ Median Family Income**

- **> 1.0** → Families are spending more than they earn (not sustainable)
- **< 1.0** → Families are living within their means

## Cost Components (EPI)
The total cost of living includes:
- Housing  
- Food  
- Transportation  
- Healthcare (including insurance premiums)  
- Childcare  
- Taxes (federal, state, local, payroll)  
- Other Necessities  

## Project Purpose
This analysis aims to provide a clearer picture of economic pressure across the U.S. Many households live paycheck to paycheck, not solely due to low income, but because essential costs have increased faster than wages. By combining cost-of-living estimates with income data, this project highlights structural affordability challenges at the county and family level.
